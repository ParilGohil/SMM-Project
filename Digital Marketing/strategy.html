// Strategy

Digital Marketing Strategy

A digital marketing strategy is a set of planned actions performed online to reach specific business goals. In simple terms, this means performing consistent actions at the right time via the most suitable online channels to increase revenue and improve relationships with your audience. Strategy is what separates reactive marketing from proactive, growth-focused campaigns.

// Competitor Analysis

How to Analyze Competitors

Competitor analysis is the process of researching and analyzing the marketing strategies of companies in your market. The research reveals what other brands are doing to acquire and retain customers, what drives traffic to their pages, and what leads to their ads. It highlights gaps and estimates your potential opportunities to fill them. Focus on 3–5 direct and indirect competitors across all digital channels.

🔍

Identify Competitors

Use tools like Semrush, Ahrefs, SimilarWeb, and Facebook Ad Library to find direct and indirect competitors. Don't forget brands targeting the same problem with different solutions.

// CRM

Customer Relationship Management

CRM in digital marketing is used to collect simple data on customers when browsing the web, then use that information to serve relevant ads when they're on social platforms. CRM enables businesses to record customer data, track sales, keep sales teams updated with customer information, improve follow-ups, connect via GPS technology, and allow team members to share files and information to ensure seamless customer experiences.

// CRM Benefits

Importance of CRM

🎯

Connected to Digital Strategy

Collect data when customers browse, then serve targeted ads when they're on Facebook or Twitter — reminding them of products they viewed and driving them back to purchase.

👤

Personalization

Use customer purchase history, browsing behavior, age, gender, and location to target specific customers with items they're likely interested in — like Netflix's "because you liked" recommendations.

🔗

Digital Connections

Create data sheets on customers, then approach them one-to-one. Send personalized email suggestions based on previous purchases and create blogs addressing their specific interests.

⭐

Satisfaction Guaranteed

Build customer service call databases to create training programs. Analyze what customers liked, disliked, and what resolved issues to consistently create satisfied customers from every interaction.

// Loyalty

Drivers of Customer Loyalty

Organizations must identify the factors that drive customer loyalty to manage it effectively. Customer attitude toward loyalty falls into three types: Emotional/Sentimental (attachment to supplier due to emotional bonds — very difficult to break), Rational (evaluates suppliers and assesses profitability — logical decision-making), and Entrepreneur (habit of trying new options — irrational and hard to retain).

🏆

Product & Services

Differentiated products reduce competition and influence customer mindset. Multiple products for the same customer enhance relationships. High service components capture more loyalty — customers avoid experimenting with competitors.

⚙️

Technology

Technological aspects of products play a vital role in loyalty. The more technologically advanced and reliable a product is, the stronger and more lasting the customer loyalty it generates.

👥

Human Resources

In sectors where customers interact directly with staff (household goods, automobiles), the capability and helpfulness of human assets directly creates positive feelings and loyalty toward the supplier.

🌟

Supplier's Culture

The most important loyalty driver. In consumer sector, culture means quality. In core sectors, it relates to technology and status. Brands like Mercedes and Ray-Ban have built cult loyalty through cultural identity and status symbolism.

// Reputation

Reputation Management in Digital Marketing

Reputation management in digital marketing is the practice of monitoring and managing a brand's image using online tactics. It allows agencies to understand how current and potential customers view a brand. Managing online reputation involves monitoring four key media categories: Owned Media (your own website, social media, blog), Earned Media (mentions, reviews on Trust Pilot, Glass Door, Google), Shared Media (social listening — what people say about your brand), and Paid Media (competitors' advertising that may affect your reputation).

🏠

Owned Media

Your own website, social media accounts, and blog. Monitoring these gives a baseline understanding of how customers interact with information put out directly by your brand.

WebsiteBlog
🏆

Earned Media

Coverage and mentions on other websites, blogs, and forums. Includes reviews on Trust Pilot, Glass Door, and Google Business. Customers research these before purchasing from your brand.

ReviewsPress
👂

Shared Media

Social listening — keeping tabs on what people say about your brand on social media from both business accounts and personal accounts. Proactive monitoring enables rapid response.

Social Listening
📢

Paid Media

Monitoring competitors' paid advertising to stay informed when other businesses attempt to affect your reputation through their advertising campaigns targeting your customers.
