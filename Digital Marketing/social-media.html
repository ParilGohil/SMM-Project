SOCIAL MEDIA MARKETING

Creating and sharing content on social networks to achieve marketing and branding goals — from brand awareness to direct sales.

// Definition

What is SMM?

Social Media Marketing (SMM) is a form of internet marketing that involves creating and sharing content on social media networks to achieve marketing and branding goals. SMM includes posting text and image updates, videos, and other content that drives audience engagement, as well as paid social media advertising. The primary goal is to produce content that users will share with their social network to increase brand exposure and broaden customer reach.

🎯

Brand Awareness

Building recognition and trust across social platforms through consistent posting, engaging content, and authentic brand voice that resonates with target audiences.

🔍

Social Media Optimization (SMO)

Like SEO for search engines, SMO is a strategy for drawing new and unique visitors to a website by adding social links (RSS feeds, sharing buttons) and promoting activity through social posts.

💬

Social CRM

SMM helps companies get direct feedback from customers while making the company seem more personable. Customers can ask questions and voice complaints through social channels.

📊

Lead Generation

Social media marketing can be used to get leads and even as a direct sales channel through promoted posts, tweets, targeted ads, and shoppable content.

// Legal Note

FTC Compliance

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has updated its rules to include SMM. If a company provides a blogger or online commenter with free products or incentives to generate positive buzz, those online comments are treated legally as endorsements. Both the blogger and the company are responsible for ensuring incentives are clearly and conspicuously disclosed, and that posts contain no misleading or unsubstantiated statements — in compliance with FTC rules concerning unfair or deceptive advertising.
