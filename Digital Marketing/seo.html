SEO & SEARCH MARKETING

Techniques that help your website rank higher in organic search results — making you visible to billions of daily searches.

// Definition

What is SEO?

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) refers to techniques that help your website rank higher in organic (or "natural") search results, thus making your website more visible to people who are looking for your product or service via search engines. It is a long-term process that requires continuous monitoring, content creation, and optimization to maintain and improve rankings.

// Process

How Search Engines Work

01
🕷️ CRAWLING

A software called a spider or crawler (like Googlebot) fetches all web pages linked to a website, systematically visiting and collecting data.

→
02
📂 INDEXING

Creating an index of all fetched pages in a giant database. Words and expressions that best describe each page are identified and assigned keywords.

→
03
⚙️ PROCESSING

When a search request arrives, the engine compares the search string with all indexed pages in the database to find matches.

→
04
📊 RELEVANCY

The search engine calculates the relevancy of each matching page to the search string using complex algorithms with hundreds of variables.

→
05
📋 RESULTS

The best-matched results are retrieved and displayed in the browser — ranked by relevance, authority, and dozens of other ranking factors.

// On-Page SEO

5 Key On-Page Optimization Elements

Each piece of content should focus on a single primary keyword or key phrase with a keyword density of 3–5% as per industry standard. Maintain a mix of primary and secondary keywords. Quality over quantity — web pages with more content depth have performed better since the 2011 Google algorithm update. Avoid keyword stuffing; write for humans first, search engines second.
Title tags contain the words in the clickable search result link. Google limits page titles to 70 characters, so they must be keyword-relevant yet concise. Place primary keywords first, then secondary keywords, and branded keywords last. This is a core part of the "big three" along with page copy and inbound links.
Meta descriptions summarize page content (155–160 characters, alphanumeric only). While they no longer directly influence rankings (since Google's 2009 declaration), they act as ad copy for organic results, influencing click-through rates. Google highlights meta description keywords that match the search phrase.
Heading tags (H1–H6) define page content section by section, like traditional headings. There should be one H1 per page containing the most relevant keyword phrase. H2 and H3 tags organize sub-sections and can include secondary keyphrases. Search engines still assign value to properly structured heading hierarchies.
Strategically linking pages within your website provides context to search engines and improves user experience. Best practices: include links in main content, use keyword-rich anchor text, avoid non-descriptive anchors like "click here", link to deep relevant pages, use breadcrumb navigation, and monitor inbound links via Google Webmaster Tools. Fewer links = more authority per link.
// Off-Page SEO

Off-Page Optimization

Off-Page SEO refers to your website's overall "authority" on the web, determined by what other websites say about your site. It's a long-term process all about your online reputation. Backlinks are the currency of any off-page strategy — focus on quality links from relevant, authoritative domains rather than quantity from unrelated sources.

🏆

Acquiring Backlinks

Getting links from higher authority sites in your niche. Search engines treat link quality as a key ranking factor — buying backlinks from unrelated domains can even penalize your site.

💬

Social Interaction

Google gives prominence to social signals: tweets, likes, shares, Google +1 votes. Your site's social footprints have a direct relation with rankings. SEO has now evolved into social.

📢

Content Promotion

Promote across Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Pinterest, and Quora. Each platform serves a different tone — keep Facebook casual, use LinkedIn for professional content, Pinterest for visual products.

// SEO Types

Types of SEO

⚙️

Technical SEO

Optimizing crawling and indexing so search engines can discover, read, and understand your website. Covers site speed, mobile-friendliness, structured data, and URL structure.

📄

Content SEO

Giving both users and search engines the content they want. Satisfying search intent with high-quality, relevant, original content that answers user questions comprehensively.

📍

Local SEO

Optimizing your website so people can find and visit your brick-and-mortar store. Includes Google My Business, local citations, and location-specific keyword targeting.

🛒

E-Commerce SEO

SEO rules specific to online stores — product page optimization, category pages, schema markup for products, and managing duplicate content across large product catalogs.

📱

Mobile SEO

Making it easy for users to find and use your website on mobile devices and tablets. Mobile-first indexing means Google primarily uses the mobile version of content.

🔗

Off-Page SEO

Promoting your website on the internet through quality backlinks, social signals, and external authority building to prove your site deserves a top spot for target keywords.

// Paid Search

Pay-Per-Click (PPC) Advertising

PPC stands for Pay-Per-Click — a model of digital advertising where the advertiser pays a fee each time one of their ads is clicked. Essentially, you're paying for targeted visits to your website. PPC ads come in different shapes and sizes, made up of text, images, videos, or combinations. They appear on search engines, websites, and social media platforms.

🎯

Bid on Keywords

Craft relevant keyword lists, tight keyword groups, and proper ad text targeting the exact search terms your customers use.

🏠

Landing Page Quality

Create optimized landing pages with persuasive, relevant content and clear calls-to-action tailored to specific search queries.

⭐

Quality Score

Google's rating of your keywords, landing pages, and campaigns. Higher Quality Scores mean more ad clicks at lower costs per click.

✨

Capture Attention

Enticing ad copy is vital. For display or social ads, eye-catching creative makes the difference between scroll-past and click-through.

// Advanced SEO

Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI)

Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI) is a method of analyzing documents to discover statistical co-occurrences of words that appear together, giving insights into the topics of those words. LSI keywords are semantically related terms that help search engines understand page context more accurately.

🛡️

Prevents Spam Labeling

Varied semantically-related keyword mentions increase content credibility. Avoids being reported as spam by users who don't find expected content.

📉

Reduces Bounce Rates

Prevents ranking for wrong search terms. If selling Lenovo notebooks, you don't want traffic from people searching for the movie "The Notebook."

🌊

Natural Content Flow

Makes content natural and meaningful without keyword stuffing. Differentiates high-quality content from keyword-dense spam content.
