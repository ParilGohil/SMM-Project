EMAIL MARKETING

One of the most effective digital channels — promoting business through targeted emails and newsletters with measurable, quantifiable results.

// Definition

What is Email Marketing?

Email marketing is promoting your business by sending overt emails and newsletters. It uses promotional letters combined with product and service details to deliver a business message to an audience. It is a cost-effective method to convey your message to highly targeted visitors. The key differentiator: email marketing is not spam — it's about companies getting in touch with people genuinely interested in their brands. Digital marketers use all other channels to add leads to email lists, then use email marketing to create customer acquisition funnels.

// Setup Guide

Setting Up an Email Marketing Campaign

01
BUILD DATABASE

Create a customer database. Buy it or generate from your website backend. Include contact information, client status, and lead source details.

→
02
CREATE CONTENT

Create a newsletter with relevant information. Set subject line, timings, and frequency. Add discounts and bonuses. Make it eye-catchy, informational, and useful.

→
03
PREPARE & TEST

Mention deal expiry date and time. Drop necessary links. Buy servers capable of bearing the load. Test email before sending — check all details, images, and links.

→
04
DEPLOY

Deploy your newsletter to your segmented list. Personalize the message for different audience segments to maximize relevance and conversion rates.

→
05
ANALYZE

Analyze performance: email delivery rate, opening rate, conversions. Use insights to optimize future campaigns and improve every metric iteratively.

// Analytics

Tracking Your Email Campaign

👆

Click-Through Rate

Gives you a clear view of how many people engage with your mail and are interested in what you offer. The most direct measure of content relevance and offer appeal.

Engagement
✅

Conversion Rate

Helps you know to what extent you are able to achieve your goals. Measures how many email recipients completed the desired action — purchase, signup, or download.

Goal Achievement
🚫

Bounce Rate

Lets you know how many consider you a spammer or time-waster. High bounce rates indicate list quality issues or sending to disengaged subscribers.

📈

Subscribers' Growth Rate

Lets you decide how much you are able to extend your reach. Measures how quickly your subscriber list is growing minus unsubscribes and bounces.

List Growth
📤

The more is the rate, the more interested your customers are. Forwarded emails generate new subscribers organically, making it a powerful virality indicator.

The gist of tracking — how many leads you generate through your campaigns and the revenue generated per dollar invested in email marketing infrastructure.

// Best Practices

Email Campaign Best Practices

Subject Line: Keep it under 50 characters. Create curiosity or urgency. Avoid spam trigger words like FREE, URGENT, ACT NOW. Personalize with recipient's name.

Segmentation: Divide your list by behavior, demographics, and purchase history. Targeted emails perform significantly better than broad blasts.

Mobile Optimization: Over 60% of emails are opened on mobile. Use responsive templates, single-column layouts, and large tap targets.

Clear CTA: Every email should have one clear call-to-action button. Multiple CTAs dilute focus and reduce conversion rates significantly.

Timing: Tuesday–Thursday mornings typically see the highest open rates. Test different send times for your specific audience segment.

A/B Testing: Test subject lines, send times, CTA text, and email design. Even small improvements compound over many campaigns.
